Abstract submission

The Complex Trait Community's 12th annual meeting will include two prominent poster sessions.

Abstracts were due 15 March 2013 and are no longer being accepted.

Abstracts from students/postdocs will be eligible for The Chicago Prize.

Conference speakers (aside from the two keynote speakers) will be chosen from the submitted abstracts. Speakers and the final program will be announced the week of 15 April 2013.

Contributed talks will be 15 or 20 minutes long, including time for questions.

Register and submit your abstract online now.

Abstracts cannot be edited once submitted. In the case of major errors in the submission process, contact CALS Conference Services at 608-263-1672 or conference@cals.wisc.edu.

Subject areas
In your abstract submission, we will ask you to choose one of the following subject areas.
  • Behavioral genetics
  • Cancer genetics
  • Complex trait analysis
  • Developmental genetics
  • Epigenetics
  • Evolutionary genetics
  • Gene expression
  • Gene-environment interactions
  • Genetic resource populations (including CC)
  • Genomics
  • High-dimensional phenotype data
  • Population genetics
  • QTL to gene
  • Statistical and computational methods
  • Systems biology
  • Translation (from model organisms to human)
